## Persona 1: Thandi — The Rider

> *"I just want to get to work without waiting an hour for a taxi that might not come."*

### Demographics

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Name** | Thandi Mokoena |
| **Age** | 28 |
| **Occupation** | Registered nurse, Phalaborwa Provincial Hospital |
| **Location** | Lulekani township, Phalaborwa |
| **Monthly income** | R8,000 |
| **Transport budget** | R1,200–R1,500/month |
| **Family status** | Single, lives with mother and younger sibling |
| **Education** | Diploma in Nursing |
| **Mobile device** | Samsung Galaxy A14 (mid-range Android) |
| **Mobile data** | R200/month prepaid (limited data — WhatsApp mostly) |

### Daily Life

Thandi works 12-hour shifts at the hospital. Her shift starts at 7:00 AM, which means she needs to leave home by 5:45 AM to account for the unpredictable taxi schedule. After a night shift ending at 7:00 PM, she faces a 20-minute walk from the taxi rank to her home in Lulekani — a walk she dreads after dark.

Her typical day:

- **05:30** — Wake up, get ready
- **05:45** — Walk to Lulekani taxi rank
- **06:00** — Wait for a shared taxi (could be 5 minutes or 40 minutes)
- **06:30** — Arrive at hospital (if taxi arrived on time)
- **17:30** — End shift, walk to taxi rank
- **18:15** — Arrive home (after taxi wait + commute)
- **18:30** — Home duties, WhatsApp with friends

### Goals & Needs

| Goal | Why It Matters |
|------|----------------|
| Book a ride quickly | She can't afford to be late for a shift — patients depend on her |
| Pay with cash | Her bank card is for savings; she budgets cash for transport |
| See driver details before riding | She needs to feel safe, especially for night shifts |
| Share ride status with mother | Her mother worries when she's late — sharing gives peace of mind |
| Track driver arrival | She can wait inside her home instead of standing at the rank |
| Schedule a ride in advance | Knowing her 5:45 AM ride is confirmed would reduce anxiety |
| Affordable fares | She can't spend more than R50 per trip on her salary |

### Pain Points

| Pain Point | Frequency | Severity |
|------------|-----------|----------|
| Taxi wait times 30–60 minutes | Daily | High |
| Night safety walking from rank home | 3–4x/week | Critical |
| No way to book in advance | Always | High |
| Fare disputes with taxi drivers | Weekly | Medium |
| Taxis don't operate during load shedding | Weekly | High |
| No way to contact driver | Always | Medium |
| Cash-only — runs out of cash mid-week | 2x/month | Medium |

### Mobile Literacy & Behavior

- **Uses daily**: WhatsApp, Facebook Lite, Google Maps (occasionally)
- **App experience**: Has used Uber once in Johannesburg; found it easy
- **Payment comfort**: Prefers cash ("I can budget better"); has used SnapScan
- **Data consciousness**: Carefully manages data; turns off mobile data when not using
- **Trust**: Needs to see driver photo, car registration, and rating before riding

### Scenario: Thandi Books a Ride

1. Opens EasyRyde rider app — home screen shows current location and search bar
2. Types "Phalaborwa Provincial Hospital" — autocomplete suggests recently used
3. Selects "Economy" ride type — sees fare estimate: R38.00
4. Taps "Request Ride"
5. Wait screen shows: "Finding driver..." with local spinner animation
6. **Within 15 seconds**: Driver assigned — Thandi sees photo, name, car model, rating (4.8)
7. Live map shows driver is 3 minutes away — she can wait inside her gate
8. Driver arrives — she confirms plate number matches — gets in
9. During ride: she taps "Share Ride" to send live location to mother on WhatsApp
10. Arrives — pays R38 cash — rate 5 stars — receipt sent via SMS (data-saver mode)

## Persona 2: Johannes — The Driver

> *"I have a good car and I know these streets. I just need a platform that sends me rides fairly and pays me on time."*

### Demographics

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Name** | Johannes Nkosi |
| **Age** | 35 |
| **Occupation** | Former taxi association driver, now looking for flexible earnings |
| **Location** | Phalaborwa CBD area |
| **Monthly income (target)** | R8,000–R12,000 |
| **Family status** | Married, two children (ages 6 and 9) |
| **Education** | Grade 12 (matric) |
| **Vehicle** | 2019 Toyota Corolla (white) — well maintained, 5-seater |
| **Mobile device** | Tecno Camon 18 (mid-range Android, dual SIM) |
| **Driving experience** | 12 years, valid SA driver's license |

### Daily Life

Johannes has been driving for the local taxi association for 6 years. He's frustrated with the high weekly fees (R1,500 to the association) and the lack of flexibility — he has to drive from 5 AM to 8 PM whether there are passengers or not. He heard about EasyRyde from a friend and sees it as a way to earn on his own terms, with higher take-home pay.

His typical current day:

- **04:30** — Report to taxi rank (pay association fee)
- **05:00** — Start driving route (Selwana → Phalaborwa CBD)
- **09:00** — Breakfast break (15 min)
- **12:00** — Lunch break (20 min)
- **16:00** — Afternoon peak
- **20:00** — End shift, return home exhausted
- **21:00** — Family time, check WhatsApp groups

### Goals & Needs

| Goal | Why It Matters |
|------|----------------|
| Set own hours | He wants to attend his children's school events |
| Keep more of what he earns | 20% to EasyRyde is better than R1,500/week fixed to association |
| Clear earnings visibility | He needs to track daily earnings in real time |
| Fair dispatch system | He wants to feel rides are distributed fairly by proximity, not favouritism |
| Cash collection option | Many riders won't have cards — he needs to collect cash in-app |
| Weekly payouts | He can't wait a month for earnings — weekly payday is critical |
| Driver support | Someone to call when there's a problem with a passenger or payment |
| See ride destination before accepting | He wants to decide if a trip is worth his time |

### Pain Points

| Pain Point | Frequency | Severity |
|------------|-----------|----------|
| Taxi association takes R1,500/week | Weekly | High |
| Long fixed hours (5AM–8PM) | Daily | High |
| No earnings tracking | Always | High |
| No breakdown of tips vs fares | Always | Medium |
| Passengers ask for app but there's none | Weekly | Medium |
| No way to rate passengers | Always | Low |
| Safety concerns with unknown passengers | Daily | Medium |
| Cash disputes with passengers | Weekly | Medium |

### Mobile Literacy & Behavior

- **Uses daily**: WhatsApp, YouTube, Facebook
- **App experience**: Has used Bolt as a passenger in Johannesburg; understands the model
- **Navigation**: Uses Google Maps regularly
- **Data**: Buys R100 data bundle every 3 days; needs data-efficient app
- **Trust**: Skeptical of new platforms — needs to see other drivers earning before fully committing

### Scenario: Johannes Drives with EasyRyde

1. **08:00** — Drops kids at school, opens EasyRyde driver app, taps "Go Online"
2. Status changes to "Online" — sees his current location on a map with nearby demand heatmap
3. **08:15** — Receives ride request notification: sound + vibration + phone screen turns on
4. Accepts — sees rider name, pickup (Lulekani Mall), drop-off (Hospital)
5. Taps "Navigate" — Google Maps opens with route
6. Arrives at pickup — rider gets in — he taps "Start Ride"
7. During ride: ignore phone, focus on driving — app auto-navigates
8. Arrives — taps "Complete Ride" — fare = R42.00
9. Rider pays cash — Johannes confirms cash receipt in app
10. Sees earnings update: "Today: R126.00 (3 rides) — Platform fee: R25.20"
11. End of week: receives payout notification — R3,200 deposited to his bank account
12. Opens earnings dashboard — sees weekly trend, compares to taxi association earnings

## Persona 3: Maria — The Admin

> *"I need one screen where I can see everything happening in the city — and act on it."*

### Demographics

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Name** | Maria du Plessis |
| **Age** | 42 |
| **Occupation** | Operations Manager, EasyRyde |
| **Location** | Phalaborwa (office near CBD) |
| **Monthly income** | R22,000 |
| **Family status** | Married, three children |
| **Education** | BCom Logistics Management |
| **Previous role** | Logistics coordinator at Palabora Mining Company |
| **Work device** | Lenovo ThinkPad + Samsung Galaxy Tab |
| **Work hours** | 6:00 AM – 5:00 PM (sometimes on call after hours) |

### Daily Work Life

Maria is the backbone of EasyRyde's operations. She manages driver onboarding, monitors live rides, handles disputes, sets pricing, and ensures the platform runs smoothly. She previously coordinated mining logistics where she managed 50+ trucks — she sees EasyRyde as a similar dispatch coordination challenge but with people.

Her typical day:

- **06:00** — Check overnight alerts: any incidents? payout failures? system anomalies?
- **07:00** — Review new driver applications (3–5 per day) — approve or request more docs
- **08:00** — Morning peak monitoring — check live ride dashboard, driver availability
- **09:00** — Standup with team (tech lead, support lead)
- **10:00** — Handle escalated support tickets (rider disputes, driver issues)
- **11:00** — Pricing review — adjust surge multipliers based on demand data
- **12:00** — Lunch + check WhatsApp community group for rider/driver feedback
- **14:00** — Afternoon peak prep — check driver coverage, trigger promo if needed
- **15:00** — Compliance review — audit driver documents, check KYC completeness
- **16:00** — Payout processing — review and approve weekly driver payouts
- **17:00** — Day wrap — review metrics, send daily ops report

### Goals & Needs

| Goal | Why It Matters |
|------|----------------|
| Live dashboard with KPIs | She needs to see rides, revenue, drivers, and issues at a glance |
| Efficient driver approval workflow | Manual approval is a bottleneck — she needs document review tools |
| Geofence-based pricing | She wants to adjust prices by zone (township vs CBD vs mine) |
| Audit trail for all actions | Compliance requires every admin action to be logged |
| Rider/driver dispute resolution tools | She needs to see chat history, ride timeline, payment details |
| Promo code management | She needs to create and track marketing promotions |
| Incident response workflow | SOS alerts must reach her immediately with full context |
| Data export for reporting | Weekly and monthly reports for stakeholders |

### Pain Points

| Pain Point | Frequency | Severity |
|------------|-----------|----------|
| Manual driver document review is tedious | Daily | High |
| No way to see system health at a glance | Daily | Critical |
| Hard to track driver issues across sessions | Weekly | High |
| No bulk action tools (suspend, verify) | Weekly | Medium |
| Reports require dev help to generate | Weekly | High |
| Can't test pricing changes without sandbox | Monthly | Medium |
| No alerting for anomalies (surge in cancellations) | Weekly | High |

### Mobile Literacy & Behavior

- **Uses daily**: Email, Slack, Google Sheets, web dashboard, WhatsApp
- **Power user**: Comfortable with complex dashboards, filtering, data export
- **Automation mindset**: Always looking for ways to reduce manual work
- **Multi-monitor**: Uses dual screens — one for monitoring, one for actions
- **After hours**: Needs mobile-accessible dashboard for on-call response

### Scenario: Maria Manages Incident Response

1. **14:30** — Maria receives SMS alert: "🚨 SOS: Rider #1042 — Selwana area"
2. Opens admin dashboard — incident section shows SOS with:
   - Rider name + phone
   - Driver name + phone + vehicle
   - Live GPS coordinates (updated every 5 seconds)
   - Ride timeline (started 12 minutes ago)
   - Chat transcript (rider: "Driver took wrong turn")
3. Calls rider — no answer
4. Calls driver — driver says rider got the address wrong, they're resolving it
5. Taps "Resolve" with notes: "Address mismatch — both parties safe"
6. Follows up 15 minutes later — ride completed normally
7. Logs incident in compliance report
8. Exports weekly incident summary: 3 SOS alerts, 0 escalations, 100% resolved within 10 minutes
